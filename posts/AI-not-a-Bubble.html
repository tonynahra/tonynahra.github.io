AI is Not a Bubble: A Defense of Foundational Technology

Challenging the fear, we show how AI's deep history and structural market growth signal a new technological age, not a speculative crash.

I. The AI Era: The Latest General-Purpose Technology (GPT)

A "bubble" implies a disconnect between valuation and underlying technological reality. We argue that the current AI surge is justified because it represents a General-Purpose Technology (GPT)—an invention so fundamental it reshapes the entire economic landscape. To dismiss AI's ascent is to misunderstand the historical pattern of foundational innovation and the enormous, necessary capital expenditure driving it.

II. AI's Deep Roots: The Historical Lens

The current technology is a culmination of decades of research, not a sudden event. Comparing AI to previous GPTs reveals striking parallels:

Key Historical Inventions (GPTs):

  • Mechanical Power: The Steam Engine led to trains, railroads, and the transformation of global transport and supply chains.
  • Electrification: Created massive new sectors for utilities, industrial manufacturing, and residential infrastructure (electricians, appliance makers).
  • Telecommunications: The Telephone and Radio/TV built vast communication networks and media empires.
  • Digitalization: The Transistor and Personal Computer powered the chip boom, leading directly to the Internet and mobile revolution.

The Deep Blue Analogy (1997):

As a chess player, I remember IBM’s Deep Blue defeating World Chess Champion Garry Kasparov in 1997. [1] This proves the concept of AI isn't new. Why the explosion now? The difference is scale, not concept. Deep Blue relied on brute force calculations using minimal memory (max 16 MB) and a single-core CPU.

III. The Engine of Change: Scale and Structural Growth

The difference between 1997 and today is the industrial-scale infrastructure buildout. This capital spending forms a structural foundation that cannot simply vanish.

The Computational Breakthrough:

  • GPU Power: Modern deep learning relies on immense, parallel processing power from companies like NVIDIA, allowing models with billions of parameters to be trained.
  • Adoption Speed: The Generative AI movement, exemplified by systems like ChatGPT, reached one million users in just five days—a pace of adoption far exceeding foundational technology phases like the Internet. [2]
  • New Platforms: Analogous to the 2007 smartphone, which launched multi-billion dollar industries (Uber, digital wallets), AI is the platform for the next wave of productivity tools and services.

Market Size and Economic Reality:

  • Market Projection: The global AI market size is projected to reach an astronomical $3.497 trillion by 2033, growing at a CAGR of 31.5% from 2025. [3]
  • Infrastructure Spending: Revenue for worldwide AI chips is set to surpass $92 billion this year, indicating massive, non-speculative capital deployment into the "picks and shovels" of the AI revolution.

IV. The Bubble Myth vs. Healthy Market Correction

Market volatility is inevitable and necessary, but the correction of speculative stocks should not be confused with the collapse of the underlying technology.

The Distinction Between Foundation and Fantasy:

  • The "Kitchen Towel" Effect: Yes, there is a bubble in minor players—companies whose only real asset is having "AI" in their name. These players are the "kitchen towel alternatives" bought in a panic, but their inevitable failure will be a return to value, not a sign of technological fraud.
  • The Dot-Com Parallel (2000): When the Dot-Com Bubble burst, the NASDAQ index fell by nearly 77%, wiping out countless unprofitable startups. [4]
    • The Survivors: Foundational companies like Microsoft, Apple, and a young Google survived and emerged stronger, having been purged of speculative competition.
    • The Legacy: The crash left behind fiber-optic cables and server farms—the essential infrastructure that powered the next two decades of digital growth.

The correction is a mechanism that filters out the hype and reinforces the true leaders (NVIDIA, Google, OpenAI, Oracle) who are built on real investment and demonstrable utility.

V. Conclusion: Be Ready for the Marvelous Age

The immense capital being deployed into AI infrastructure is not speculative; it is a long-term, tectonic shift. We are witnessing the foundation being laid for an amazing new era—one promising robots that read your mind, algorithms that know you better than you know yourself, and efficiencies that will fundamentally redefine productivity across every industry. The market may fluctuate, but the technological direction is irreversible. Be ready for the marvelous age.
